## Per-App Breakdown

### 1. Speeko (4.73) — Dominant #1

**Strengths:**
- Roger Love (celebrity vocal coach) content — strong authority signal
- 2-minute daily exercises — low friction habit loop
- Speech stats tracking (pace, filler words, energy)
- Local storage (no cloud dependency)
- Ranks #1 on 3 of 8 keywords, top 4 on all 8

**Weaknesses:**
- $19.99/month — most expensive in category, frequent complaint
- Accuracy doubts — users question AI pitch/tone feedback reliability
- Generic feedback — "not personalised enough"
- No real conversation practice

---

### 2. Orai (4.61) — Lowest-rated incumbent

**Strengths:**
- Detailed filler word counting (um, uh, like, you know)
- Comprehensive speech analysis metrics
- Used by Toastmasters members

**Weaknesses:**
- Pause detection broken — misreads deliberate pauses as filler
- Server errors and 1hr+ loading freezes
- Clunky, dated UI
- Lowest rating of any direct competitor (4.61)
- App feels abandoned — slow update cadence

---

### 3. Vocal Image (4.52) — AI roleplay focus

**Strengths:**
- 10-day visible improvement reported by users
- Bite-sized sessions
- AI roleplay scenarios (job interviews, meetings)
- Broad coaching: tone, pace, clarity

**Weaknesses:**
- Tells users WHAT is wrong but not HOW to fix it
- Accent/dialect struggles — non-American users penalised
- "AI slop" updates — recent changes feel generated, not curated
- Billing issues — users report unexpected charges
- Lowest overall rating (4.52)

---

### 4. Vocabulary (4.79) — Not a true competitor

**Strengths:**
- High rating (4.79), 3M+ downloads
- Widget for daily word learning
- Simple, addictive UX

**Weaknesses:**

- Ranking for speaking keywords is incidental (ASO overlap)
- Aggressive paywall — custom word lists locked behind sub
- Broken widget — words don't refresh
- Loud, frequent ads on free tier

**Note:** Vocabulary is not a direct competitor. Ranks due to keyword overlap, not feature overlap.

## Feature Opportunities

Based on competitor gaps, here's what we could build to differentiate:

### 1. "Fix It" Coaching (High Priority)
**Gap:** Apps diagnose problems but don't teach fixes.
**Opportunity:** After detecting "too many filler words," show a specific drill: "Try the 2-second pause technique. Here's a 30-second exercise..." Every diagnosis links to a targeted micro-exercise.

### 2. Accent-Agnostic AI (High Priority)
**Gap:** Most apps only work well for American English speakers.
**Opportunity:** Support British, Australian, Indian, and other English accents. UK-based developer (us) = natural credibility for non-US accents. Massive underserved market.

### 3. Transparent, Fair Pricing (High Priority)
**Gap:** Billing complaints plague ELSA, Vocal Image, Vocabulary.
**Opportunity:** Clear pricing, easy cancellation, no dark patterns. Hard paywall with generous free trial (per CLAUDE.md rules). Make trust a selling point in screenshots.

### 4. Spontaneous Speaking Mode (Medium Priority)
**Gap:** All competitors use scripted prompts only.
**Opportunity:** Random topic generator ("Explain why dogs are better than cats in 60 seconds") with real-time AI feedback. Simulates actual public speaking pressure.

### 5. Fast Time-to-Value Onboarding (Medium Priority)
**Gap:** ELSA and Orai have painful onboarding flows.
**Opportunity:** First speech analysis within 30 seconds of opening app. No account required to try. Show immediate value, THEN ask for signup.

### 6. Progress Streaks + Social Proof (Medium Priority)
**Gap:** Most apps have weak habit loops beyond Speeko.
**Opportunity:** Daily streak, weekly improvement graph, shareable "I practiced X minutes this week" cards. Gamification that drives retention.

### 7. Presentation Mode (Lower Priority)
**Gap:** No app focuses specifically on slide-deck presentations.
**Opportunity:** Timer + slide-cue feature — practice a 5-minute talk with slide prompts, get feedback on pacing per slide. Business users would pay for this.

### 8. Offline-First Architecture (Lower Priority)
**Gap:** Orai has server dependency issues, others require internet.
**Opportunity:** Core speech analysis works offline. AsyncStorage for all user data (per CLAUDE.md rules). Sync when connected.

## Strategic Summary

**Market position:** The public speaking app space has a clear #1 (Speeko) but no dominant, well-rounded solution. Most competitors are either:
- Speech apps that don't teach you to fix problems (Speeko, Vocal Image)
- Accent/pronunciation apps ranking for speaking keywords but not actually competing (ELSA, BoldVoice, Accent Training, Vocabulary)
- Broken or abandoned apps (Orai, Astound)

**Our angle:** A public speaking coach that actually teaches you HOW to improve, works for any English accent, has transparent pricing, and delivers value in under 30 seconds. Only 4 of the top 10 are true public speaking apps (Speeko, Orai, Vocal Image, PatterAI) — and all 4 have significant weaknesses.

**Best keywords to target:** "speech training" (diff 32), "presentation practice" (diff 35), "speech practice" (diff 36). Avoid "public speaking" (diff 50) and "public speaking ai" (diff 50) initially.
